The earliest version of this picture, called 'The Mass of St. Gregory' is found in the 8th-century biography of Gregory by Paul the Deacon, and was repeated in the 9th-century one by John the Deacon. In this version, the Pope was saying Mass when a woman present started to laugh at the time of the Communion, saying to a companion that she could not believe the bread was Christ, as she herself had baked it. Gregory prayed for a sign, after which the bread turned into a visionary appearance of the whole of Christ on the altar.
This certain description, which was created from feathers on a wood panel, often used by the Aztecs in the 1500s. This piece of work is the oldest dated feather work with a Christian subject. This was created by the Aztecs in order for a way to worship the Lord from the knowledge they knew of Christ.
